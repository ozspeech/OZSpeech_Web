OZSpeech: One-step Zero-shot Speech Synthesis with Learned-Prior-Conditioned Flow Matching

Abstract

Text-to-speech (TTS) systems have seen significant advancements in recent years, driven by improvements in deep learning and neural network architectures. This paper introduces OZSpeech, the first TTS method that explores optimal transport conditional flow matching with one-step sampling and a learned prior as the condition. Instead of using traditional speech representations such as waveforms or spectrograms, this method operates on disentangled factorized components of speech in token format. Experiment results show that our method achieves superior performance over existing methods in content accuracy, naturalness, prosody generation, and speaker style preservation.
